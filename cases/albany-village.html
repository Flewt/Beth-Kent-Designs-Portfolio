Overview

The Brief

Designing a solution for a pain point within our community.

The Problem

Once a bustling centre of activity, Albany Village has become nothing more than a congestion footnote on a commuter's trip north-west. Despite Albany being one of the fastest growing suburbs in Auckland, development has focused around Westfield Albany mall. Albany Village still boasts some local attractions, such as a lovely park, the Albany Library, and a Chinese community centre.

The Challenge

Albany residents do not have a local community space outside of the mall. My challenge was to design a creative media strategy that would bring new life to the old Albany Village.

The Solution

A two-part campaign that starts with a renovation of the village, followed by a community campaign to revitalise local interest in Albany Village.

Target Audience

The target demographic for this project is local adults with children between 5–12 years old.

Jenny Chen

43 · Homemaker

Jenny is a homemaker who moved to New Zealand with her husband about 12 years ago. She is dedicated to her children's (aged 7 and 10) education and is actively involved in community activities for the local Chinese population. She takes her kids to the library for the Chinese read-along, but otherwise prefers to go further afield for shopping and activities.

Amanda Davis

34 · Single Mother

Amanda is a single mother with a busy, time-intensive career and a 5-year-old daughter. She wishes there was somewhere close to home that is safe, where she can let her daughter play while she gets some "me time."

Reflection

Reflections & Learnings

This project was a huge learning opportunity where I got to practice identifying pain points that people encounter in their day to day. Getting to design the brand, campaign, and website all in once project was very exciting. I learnt a lot about the value of lo-fi prototyping and now spend a lot more time getting the lo-fi right before moving onto the hi-fi and final design.
